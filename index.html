The Problem
Plastic Pollution Around the World -  Every day, more than 60 million plastic bottles end up in landfills. And that number is still growing. This excess of plastic has been damaging ecosystems, taking up land, and poisoning the Earth. As members of our planet, we have a responsibility to preserve our world. Here is our solution to this problem.
Americans only recycle 9% of all plastics.
Our Idea
The mealworm uses Enterobacter asburiae to digest plastic.
Meet the Mealworm.  Armed with an incredible ability to digest plastic, this arthropod doesn't just remove waste - it also converts it to biodegradeable soil usable for crops. However, these insects can only digest a little bit of plastic at a time - around 0.36 mg a day. Our team realized that these bugs could use a boost to reach their plastic-eating potential.
pBot
The Next Big Thing.  Equipped with cutting edge technology, the pBot can traverse the difficult terrain of landfills to find and collect the plastic in them. It uses bacteria from the mealworm to convert this plastic into biodegradable soil, which is useful in agricultural settings.
Infrared Sensors are used to detect objects in front of pBot.
